About

Paramore (pronounced "pa-ra-more") is an independent, 3x Grammy-winner, American rock band that formed in Franklin, Tennessee in 2004. Paramore currently consists of lead vocalist Hayley Williams, guitarist Taylor York, and drummer Zac Farro. Paramore's former members include Josh Farro, Jason Bynum, John Hembree, Jeremy Davis, and Hunter Lamb.

History

In 2002, Hayley Williams who was 13 at the time, moved from Meridian, Mississippi to Franklin, Tennessee, where she met Josh and Zac Farro on school. Alongside Jeremy Davis and Kimee Read. They were part of a funk cover band, while Josh and Zac had daily practice sessions. Despite initial uneasiness about having a female singer, Williams started writing for the group anyway, which led to the official formation of Paramore in 2004. Paramore during this time comprised of Josh, Zac, Jeremy, and herself, which later joined by Jason Bynum. Despite doubts about Zac's age because he's still so young, their talent was amazing. The name "Paramore" came from a former bassist's mother's maiden name, which later discovered to be a homophone for "paramour". They produced their debut song, "Conspiracy", and performed at various venues. This caught the attention of John Janick, which led to their signing with Fueled by Ramen in April 2005, after a performance in Orlando, Florida.

Influences

Paramore's music, which is sometimes categorized as emo and pop-punk, has been praised for its maturity and honesty. Some critics even putting a highlight on a change of Paramore's style and genre which makes them different from other stereotypes. Their debut album, "All We Know is Falling," while some considered somewhat formulaic and textbook, still showcased and demonstrated their talent in delivering well produced pop-punk album. Paramore explored their style more on "Riot!". This album expanded their sound and energy while staying true to their roots. Paramore also strayed from its sound on the album "After Laughter" which started with controversy. This album marked the time where Paramore undergoes the most drastic change in sound and style. Paramore was Influenced by bands like Blink-182 and Jimmy Eat World. Paramore aims for a career similar to U2 and No Doubt, who emphasized on artistic integrity and a good connection with their fans. All Paramore's members identify as Christian, with Josh Farro even highlighting the importance of faith in their music without any proselytizing. Instead Farro stressed to express their love for music as their primary motivation.
